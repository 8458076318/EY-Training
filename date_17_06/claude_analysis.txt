Task 1 — Financial Analysis
What should an analyst flag?
Despite 12% revenue growth, several red flags warrant attention. 
The gross margin compression from 68% to 61% (a 7-point drop) is significant — it suggests either rising cost of goods, pricing pressure, or that AI infrastructure costs are flowing through COGS. Operating expenses rising 31% far outpaces revenue growth of 12%, meaning the company is spending its way to growth rather than scaling efficiently. The CFO's mention of "AI infrastructure investment" may be the explanation, but it raises the question of whether these are one-time capex items or recurring opex, and whether they're being capitalized or expensed. The CEO's "on track" comment is vague — analysts should push for specific guidance on when margins are expected to recover. Net income and free cash flow figures are absent from the summary, which are critical to assess true health. Verdict: Not a healthy quarter on fundamentals — growth is masking deteriorating profitability.

Task 2 — Customer Return Decision
Should you accept the return?
Yes, you should accept the return, and here's the reasoning: while the purchase was technically 47 days ago — two days past the 45-day policy — the customer reported the zipper broke on day 44, meaning the defect occurred within the return window, which shifts this from a policy question to a product quality issue. The customer's history matters here: 23 orders over 6 years signals genuine loyalty, and rigidly enforcing a 2-day overage risks losing a high-lifetime-value customer over what is likely a manufacturing defect. The order is verifiable in the system, removing fraud risk. A goodwill exception is warranted — accept the return, note it as a quality defect, and flag the zipper issue to the product team.
